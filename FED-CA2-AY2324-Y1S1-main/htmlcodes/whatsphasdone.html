What has SP done to save the enviroment?

Well, below here are some of the things SP has done!

What has SP done?

SP Enviromental Club

SP has its own enviromental club which is a cca group commited to promoting enviromental awareness and a sustainable lifestyle on campus and beyond.

SP enviromental club hosts events such as "Recycle potted plant", which is a event where you bring your own plastic bottle and they will guide you on how to grow a plant inside the bottle.
SP enviromental club also makes informative posters such as the '4 Plastic Donation Rules' where they teach you what are the steps to follow before donating your plastic.

SP enviromental club has it's own social media account on Instagram @sp_env. Do check out their social media for upcoming events regarding to enviromental sustainability!

Our Pledge

We pledge to green our campus, reduce our enviromental impact and nurture stewards of sustainability.

Nurture stewards of the environment

It is our mission, as a polytechnic for all ages, to prepare our learners to be life-ready, work-ready and world-ready for the transformation of Singapore. By incorporating modules on sustainability into our curriculum, we hope to sow the seeds that will grow into future stewards of the environment.
SP students are introduced to the SG Green Plan and the United Nations’ Sustainable Development Goals through our Common Core Curriculum. We also offer sustainability-related modules in our full-time diplomas and Continuing Education and Training programmes.

Spreading joy through green spaces

We believe in creating environments that support people physically, emotionally and culturally—to enhance the overall mental health and well-being of our community. In a bid to design more liveable spaces, we have installed smart sensors that track indoor air quality and human traffic to keep our surroundings comfy and occupants happy.

Harness green partnerships for our future

We believe in the importance of working together to push the green agenda forward. Our partnerships, with industry leaders such as Grundfos, Danfoss and Singapore Green Building Council, go beyond developing sustainable solutions for local industries. We continually strive to upskill the Singapore workforce on sustainable business practices.
One such project involves partnering Samwoh, a leading integrated construction company, to develop a sustainable solution for our roads and pavements by using recycled plastic to create a more durable surface.

Rally the community to amplify impact

Our journey towards a more sustainable future starts from within. The SP Environment Club is a student club that aims to drive environmental consciousness throughout campus with a variety of campaigns, competitions and social media content.

Cherish our natural resources

We believe that it is our duty to conserve natural resources for the future. Starting with conscious consumption, we have ceased using bottled water at events to reduce plastic waste on campus. In 2023, we will install photo-voltaic panels across campus to harness renewable clean energy.

Study, work and play among lush greenery

Taking a stroll along one of SP's many footpaths, you would find yourself immersed in greenery and nature—promoting relaxation, reducing stress and anxiety, and providing an instant mood boost.
When the foundation stone of our beautiful campus was laid, we have sought to integrate our buildings with its natural surroundings rather than replace them. We have also documented 230 species of flora on campus including our majestic Brown Woolly Fig Heritage Trees found near Food Court 1.

Go Green SP

At the Go Green SP event held on Wednesday, 5 July 2023, PCEO Mr. Soh Wai Wah led an SP pledge with students and staff for our commitment and support towards the nation-wide sustainability movement. The event was graced by Senior Minister of State, Dr. Amy Khor, who led a panel discussion on Singapore's commitment to achieve net zero emissions by 2050, joined by guest speakers from various organizations.
